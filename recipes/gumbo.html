Chicken and Sausage Gumbo

Description

description of gumbo

Ingredients

    For the Roux:

  • 2 1/2 oz (74g) all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup (130 ml) + 1 tbsp. grapeseed oil or high smoke point oil

    • For the Gumbo

  • 1 lb. andouille sausage, cut into coins
  • 1 lb. chicken thighs
  • 6 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 2 celery ribs, diced
  • 1 small green bell pepper, diced
  • 1 jalapeño, seeded and diced
  • 1 cup (250 ml) amber-style beer
  • 4 cups chicken stock
  • 1 tsp. thyme
  • 4 bay leaves
  • salt, to taste
  • 1 tsp. black pepper, plus more to taste

    • To Serve:

  • cooked white rice, for serving
  • scallions, for serving

Steps

  1. Season chicken with salt, black pepper, and 1 tbsp. of grapeseed oil. Broil it until slightly charred and golden, about 8 minutes on each side, and set aside.

    2. NOTE: Make sure that all the veggies are prepped, beer and stock are nearby, and you do not walk away from the roux.

  2. Heat a thick bottomed 4-quart Dutch oven over medium. Add remaining oil and wait until it starts to smoke. Add in flour, using a whisk, stir constantly. Roux is ready when it has turned dark brown (the color of a Hershey's chocolate is about right), about 25 minutes.
  3. Add the celery, japaleño, bell pepper, and onion for 2 minutes. Add the garlic and bay leaves for 1 minute. Once the onions turn transluscent, add the beer to deglaze, then add the stock, thyme, and 1 tsp. of black pepper.
  4. Stir slowly until the gumbo is at a simmer, then add the chicken and sausage. Bring back to a simmer and cover, stirring occasionally for 2 1/2 to 3 hours. Gumbo should be thick, but not like gravy.
  5. Serve over cooked white rice, topped with scallions, and enjoy!
